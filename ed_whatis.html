What is the Department of Education?
The Department of Education handles the vast majority of policy and federal assistance as it pertains to education:
  • It facilitates the President's education policy and helps execute laws enacted by Congress.
  • It strives to ensure America is competitively positioned on a global scale by fostering educational excellence.
  • It works toward the goal of equal access to education for all Americans.

The Department of Education serves Americans of all ages:
  • The elementary and secondary program serves approximately 55 million students. (Pre-K through Grade 12)
    • 100,000 Public Schools
    • 34,000 Private Schools
  • The department also serves 10 million Undergraduate students.
    • Grant, loan, and work-study assistance
What is not the Department of Education's role?
DoEd is not resposible for the following:
  • Establishing schools and colleges
  • Developing curriculum
  • Determining requirements for enrollment or graduation
  • Setting state education standards
  • Implementing tests to determine if states are meeting their standards
